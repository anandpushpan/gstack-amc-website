# v2.8 Changes: Remove the "newly launched / real reviews only" placeholder

**Date**: 2026-06-13

## Why

The trust section ended with a dashed-border block headed "Real reviews only"
that said we are "newly launched in the UK". The intent was honesty about having
no reviews yet, but it backfired: announcing newness gives a visitor a reason to
hesitate ("why try them over an established provider?") without adding any trust
in return.

## What changed

- Removed the `.reviews-honest` block (grey stars, "Real reviews only" heading,
  "newly launched" paragraph) from the trust section.
- Removed the now-unused `.reviews-honest` / `.stars` CSS.
- Kept a dev comment marking where a Trustpilot embed goes once a real profile
  exists, and restating the rule: no ratings, counts or testimonials until real
  and verifiable.

The trust section now ends on its four verifiable cards: official data sources
(DVLA / DVSA), OEM build and service data, the UK-registered company with its VAT
number, and secure payment / refund terms. These carry the trust without
flagging newness.

The structured data was already free of AggregateRating, so no schema change was
needed.

## Verification

- 0 `.reviews-honest` nodes remain in the rendered page.
- Trust section renders complete with the four cards, no empty space.
- 0 em/en dashes. Desktop and section layout unaffected elsewhere.
