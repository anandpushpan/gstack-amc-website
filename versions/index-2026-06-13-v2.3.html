Factory data others can't access

Service and build records that prove the story

Most checks scrape public MOT records. We tap OEM (factory) databases for the complete picture.

Digital service book, not owner guesses

We pull official service records from the factory dealer network. You see every stamp: date, mileage, service type, and which dealer did the work. No owner's diary entries, no photo evidence needed.

  • OEM records only: from the manufacturer, not the owner
  • Timeline view: spot missed services at a glance
  • Trust signal: main dealer work costs more than you'd pay, so it was done

Build sheet: what it came with

The factory build sheet lists every option the car left the plant with: engine, transmission, trim, safety pack, tech package. This matters for insurance group, fuel economy, and resale. Most checks don't bother showing it.

  • Insurance group: confirm before you commit
  • Emissions standard: which tax band, real fuel economy
  • Spec verification: does the car match the listing?

From history to numbers

MOT trends and valuations that move with the market

You can see a car's history anywhere. What you need is what it tells you about the future.

MOT history with clocking detection

Every MOT test result is here: pass, advisory, fail. But the chart is what matters: we plot mileage over time so spikes and reversals jump out. Clocked cars show a sudden dip. You spot it in seconds.

  • Full test log: every MOT pass, advisory, and failure since registration
  • Clocking flags: automatic detection when mileage dips
  • Repair patterns: recurring failures = bigger problems ahead

Grade-based valuations with projections

We don't give you one magic number. You see the market value for a Clean, Average, and Below-Average version of this car. Then we project where the price goes over the next 12 and 24 months, so you know if you're buying at the peak or the trough.

  • Three grades shown: the range you're negotiating within
  • Live market data: updated daily from auction and retail transactions
  • Future projections: what will it be worth in a year?

Yes. Your first full report is free and includes everything except the AI Visual Assessment: vehicle overview, MOT and mileage, digital service book, grade-based valuation and the build sheet. No card needed to run it.

Vehicle overview and A-C condition grade, full MOT and mileage history (with clocking flags), OEM digital service book, factory build sheet and spec, a grade-based market valuation with projections, and an AI visual condition assessment with itemised repair costs. Sourced from DVLA, DVSA and OEM data.
